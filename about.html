Clarity.
Narrative.
Brand Architect.

I build brands by cutting through what doesn't matter and getting to what does.

A lot of how I think comes down to pattern recognition, backed by data. Most brands don't struggle with creativity as much as they struggle with clarity. The work is knowing what matters, how to express it, and making sure it still holds up once it's out in the world.

More often than not, the issue isn't a lack of ideas. It's too many of them. Things get layered, diluted, or shaped by systems that no longer work. I sort through that, get to the core, and shape something people can recognize and respond to. From there, I build the structure that carries it across channels and allows it to scale.

I started in the arts. I studied how fan culture shapes economies at the graduate level, then co-founded a contemporary arts organization. In 2016, I made the Nasty Woman T-shirt from my living room. It went globally viral within a day and became the foundation of Shrill Society, a self-funded business I built and ran for five years. That experience continues to shape how I think about audience, ownership, and what brands owe the people who engage with them.

Since then, I've applied the same approach inside larger organizations. Today, I'm VP of Content and Brand Marketing at ANDMORE, a Blackstone portfolio company and one of North America's largest wholesale marketplace operators. I oversee campaign and narrative strategy across multiple markets and audiences, translating business priorities into work that is clear, cohesive, and built to perform.

I'm based in New Orleans, where storytelling is part of the air. That influence carries into my work as the founder of Strange Weather, a fragrance house rooted in local mythology and lore, built around the idea that scent can function as atmospheric storytelling.

At the center of it all is a simple focus: understanding what matters, shaping it into something people recognize, and building it to last.
