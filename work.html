ANDMORE VP, Content & Brand Marketing 2022 – Present

ANDMORE is one of North America's largest wholesale marketplace operators, running physical markets and design centers across the home, gift, furniture, apparel, and design industries. As VP of Content and Brand Marketing, I lead enterprise-level brand narrative, content strategy, and integrated GTM marketing across a portfolio of 30 markets — spanning 15 websites, 19 print publications, and lifecycle and email programs reaching hundreds of thousands of buyers and exhibitors globally.

My work centers on building cohesive narrative infrastructure across a matrixed organization where each market has its own identity, buyer community, and stakeholder set. That means developing campaign strategy from scratch, establishing the messaging hierarchies that hold across 30 distinct market identities, and influencing editorial, creative, product, sales, and executive teams — up, down, and laterally — to maintain a unified brand story without erasing what makes each market its own. I personally own storytelling and campaign development, and serve as executive producer of The Market Makers, the company's CEO-led podcast.

The Market Makers has seen 189% organic growth in monthly downloads, exceeds all B2B podcast benchmarks, and reaches listeners across the US, Canada, the UK, Singapore, and India — built with no paid distribution. Recent campaigns drove the largest Atlanta Market attendance in two years, with first-time buyer growth of 15% and a 36% increase in hospitality and event planning attendance. Las Vegas Market's Winter 2026 campaign brought 29% new-to-ANDMORE attendees, with 1.4M+ estimated earned media reach across 26 placements and 66 press attendees from 45 outlets.

Shrill Society Founder & Creative Director 2016 – 2021

Founded and built Shrill Society as a direct-to-consumer impact lifestyle brand with no outside capital and no debt. The brand gained international recognition for the Nasty Woman campaign — a single product concept that became a global cultural moment and went viral three times, ultimately generating a seven-figure business from a standing start.

Every element of the brand was designed and executed in-house: creative direction, product development, content strategy, PR, partnership management, and digital audience architecture. The Nasty Woman Game was acquired and published by Penguin Random House. The brand raised over $137,000 for Planned Parenthood, the ACLU, and Sister Song through product-tied giving programs. International licensing agreements expanded distribution across multiple countries.

Media coverage included the New York Times, Forbes, The Washington Post, The Cut, Teen Vogue, Allure, and Glamour, among 300+ total placements.

Pelican Bomb Co-founder, Creative & Operations Director 2011 – 2018

Co-founded Pelican Bomb as a nonprofit contemporary visual arts organization in New Orleans, building it over seven years into a nationally recognized institution. Programs included an online publication read in all 50 states and 10 countries, a physical gallery space, a Critic-in-Residence program supported by the National Endowment for the Arts, and a Community Supported Art initiative that was replicated in over 80 cities worldwide.

As Creative and Operations Director, I oversaw all programming, brand and editorial identity, revenue-generating initiatives, and creative partnerships — including co-productions with Hilton Hotels, the Downtown Development District, and major cultural institutions. The organization raised over $200,000 directly benefiting New Orleans artists, writers, and curators over its seven-year run.
