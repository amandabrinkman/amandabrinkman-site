Keynotes, panels,
and fireside conversations.

I've presented at SXSW, OMR Hamburg, Duke University, Tulane University, the University of Texas at Austin, University of Pennsylvania, Vanderbilt University, American University, Red Bull, the School of the Art Institute of Chicago, and the Contemporary Arts Center New Orleans, among others.

My talks focus on what it takes to build brands that break through, the mechanics of cultural resonance, and the difference between content that performs once and narrative that compounds. I teach undergraduate courses on pop culture, fan culture, and contemporary media studies at SAIC and the University of New Orleans.

Talk Topics
Building Brands That Break Through

How I built Shrill Society from a single viral moment into a seven-figure brand — the decisions, the failures, and the specific principles that made it repeatable.

GTM Narrative: The Story Before the Launch

Why most go-to-market strategies fail at the narrative level — and how to build positioning and story architecture that holds under pressure and across audiences.

Culture as Strategy

How brands earn cultural relevance versus buying it, and what it takes to make work that people share because it means something to them.

Workshop: Finding Your Brand's Real Story

An interactive session for teams working through positioning, narrative, or launch strategy. Participants leave with sharper language and a clearer point of view.

AI-Native Brand Operations

How brand and content teams can build AI into their workflows without losing creative quality or strategic clarity. Practical frameworks for VP-level leaders managing complex content operations at scale.
