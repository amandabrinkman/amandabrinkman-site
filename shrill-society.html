Chief Brand & Marketing Officer · 2016–2021

Shrill Society

Founded and built Shrill Society as a direct-to-consumer impact lifestyle brand with no outside capital and no debt. The brand gained international recognition for the Nasty Woman campaign — a single product concept that became a global cultural moment and went viral three times, ultimately generating a seven-figure business from a standing start. Every element of the brand was designed and executed in-house: creative direction, product development, content strategy, PR, partnership management, and digital audience architecture.

Nasty Woman T-Shirt

Creative Direction · Design · Cross-Platform Marketing · PR Management

On October 19, 2016, I watched the presidential debate and heard Trump say "such a nasty woman" about Clinton. I mocked up the shirt, posted it to Instagram, and woke up to 20,000 emails and 10,000 orders by 9 AM. The design went globally viral three times and raised over $137k for Planned Parenthood, the ACLU, and Sister Song.

Nasty Woman Game

Original Concept · Creative Direction · Production · Integrated Marketing · Event Production

A social party game published by Penguin Random House, released August 2018 and followed by a US game tour. Players say what they really think through a series of prompts — comedic, serious, or irreverent depending on who's playing. Design by Maria Hinds. Illustrations by Erin K. Wilson.

Nasty Woman Planner

Original Concept · Creative Direction · Production · Integrated Marketing

Published in 2017 and 2018. An interactive daily planner with monthly and weekly calendars, original content, illustrations, and prompts, complemented by digital downloads and interviews. Built in partnership with brand sponsors, nonprofits, and media partners.

The Shout

Original Concept · Creative Direction · Content · Community

A bi-weekly newsletter covering what we were reading, watching, buying, and attending. No promotion, no paid acquisition. Grew entirely by word of mouth to 15,000 subscribers.
